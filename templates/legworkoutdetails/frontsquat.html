Front Squat

Description

The front squat is a powerful compound lift that primarily targets the quadriceps while also engaging the core, glutes, and upper back. It places the barbell on the front of the shoulders, requiring greater upright posture and core activation compared to the back squat.

Posture Guide

- Rest the barbell on the front delts with elbows high and parallel to the ground
- Keep chest up and spine neutral
- Stand with feet shoulder-width apart and toes slightly out
- Lower into a squat by bending hips and knees while keeping torso upright
- Push through heels to return to standing while maintaining elbow height
